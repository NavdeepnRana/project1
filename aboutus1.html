Guidance for Mental Well-being


Anxiety

Anxiety is a feeling of fear, dread, and uneasiness. It might cause you to sweat, feel restless and tense, and have a rapid heartbeat. It can be a normal reaction to stress. For example, you might feel anxious when faced with a difficult problem at work, before taking a test, or before making an important decision.

Depression

Depression is a serious mental illness that can cause a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. It can affect how you think, feel, and behave. Depression can also lead to physical problems.

Depression is not just a fleeting feeling of sadness or a temporary response to life's challenges. It is a complex condition that can affect every aspect of a person's life, including their physical health, relationships, and ability to perform daily tasks. Symptoms of depression can vary widely but often include feelings of hopelessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, changes in appetite, and sleep disturbances.

Addiction

Addiction is a condition where a person cannot stop using a substance or engaging in a behavior, even when it causes harm. It affects the brain’s reward system, making the person crave the substance or activity despite negative consequences.

Insomnia

Insomnia is a sleep disorder where a person has trouble falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking up too early and not being able to sleep again. It can make a person feel tired, irritated, and unable to focus during the day.

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar Disorder is a mental health condition that causes extreme mood swings between two opposite states:

1. Mania (High Energy & Excitement) – Feeling overly happy, energetic, or restless.

2. Depression (Extreme Sadness & Low Energy) – Feeling very sad, tired, and hopeless.

Personality Disorder

Personality Disorder is a type of mental health condition where a person has long-term patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving that are significantly different from societal expectations. These patterns can cause distress or problems in relationships, work, and daily life.
